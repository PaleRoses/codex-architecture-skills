# Quality Gates

1. The index structure is named.
2. Arrows are named in domain language and their direction is explicit.
3. The selected registry structure is the weakest lawful structure that preserves the semantics.
4. Lookup semantics are not confused with restriction, transport, or gluing.
5. Construction errors cover duplicate keys, unknown objects, broken morphisms, and conflict cases.
6. Identity and composition laws are testable.
7. Presheaf designs test restriction over composed arrows.
8. Sheaf designs define overlaps, mismatch type, matching family, gluing operation, and uniqueness policy.
9. Fibred designs define fibers and reindexing.
10. There is no global mutable singleton, silent override chain, or raw string map as the canonical semantics.
